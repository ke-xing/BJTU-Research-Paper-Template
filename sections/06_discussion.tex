\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{Why does the curriculum help so much?} We hypothesize that
gradually increasing problem difficulty prevents the model from memorizing
shortcut heuristics; ablation in Table~\ref{tab:ablation} supports this.

\begin{limitations}
BJTU-LLM has three known limitations. \textbf{(i)}~We evaluate only on
English benchmarks; multilingual reasoning is left for future work.
\textbf{(ii)}~Our sparse attention degrades slightly on very short sequences
($N<128$). \textbf{(iii)}~The process-reward model requires an additional
2\,B tokens of preference data, which may not be available in all domains.
\end{limitations}
