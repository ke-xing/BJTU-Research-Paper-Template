\begin{abstract}
Large language models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable reasoning abilities
when scaled to hundreds of billions of parameters, yet the training cost
remains prohibitive for most academic institutions. We present
\textbf{BJTU-LLM}, a family of 1.5B / 7B / 13B parameter models that match the
reasoning performance of models $5\times$ their size on standard benchmarks.
Our approach combines (i) a curriculum-based synthetic-data pipeline, (ii) a
hardware-aware sparse attention operator, and (iii) a two-stage RLHF procedure
with process-level rewards. On GSM8K, MATH, and HumanEval, BJTU-LLM-7B achieves
$82.4$\%, $54.7$\%, and $71.3$\% respectively, outperforming Llama-3-8B by
$+6.1$, $+8.3$, and $+4.2$ points. We release all model weights, training data
recipes, and evaluation code to the community.
\end{abstract}

\begin{tldr}
A recipe for training compact but strong reasoning LLMs at a fraction of the
compute of frontier labs. \textbf{7B model matches 70B model on math.}
Weights, data, and code are open-sourced.
\end{tldr}
